Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about dental care, appointments, and our services

How often should I visit the dentist?

Most patients benefit from a cleaning and exam every six months. If you have gum disease or other concerns, we may recommend more frequent visits.

What should I do if I have a dental emergency?

Call us immediately. We reserve same-day appointments for emergencies like toothaches, swelling, broken teeth, or injuries.

Does teeth whitening hurt?

Professional whitening is safe and effective. Some patients may notice temporary sensitivity, which usually resolves in 24–48 hours.

What's the difference between a crown, bridge, and implant?

• Crown: A "cap" placed over a damaged tooth.
• Bridge: Replaces one or more missing teeth by attaching to nearby teeth.
• Implant: A titanium post surgically placed into the jaw with a crown on top — the closest replacement to a natural tooth.

At what age should my child see the dentist?

By age 1, or within six months of their first tooth. Early visits help prevent issues and build comfort with dental care.

Will a root canal hurt?

No. Root canals relieve pain caused by infection. With modern anesthesia and techniques, treatment feels much like a standard filling.

What if I'm nervous about going to the dentist?

You're not alone. We take extra time to explain your care, answer questions, and provide comfort options to make your visit stress-free.

Do you see children and families?

Yes! We welcome patients of all ages, from toddlers to seniors. We love helping children build healthy habits early.

Do you take walk-ins?

While appointments are encouraged, we do our best to accommodate same-day walk-ins, especially for emergencies.

How much does treatment cost?

Every patient's needs are different. During your visit, we'll provide a personalized treatment plan with upfront cost estimates, insurance benefits, and financing options.

What if I haven't been to the dentist in years?

That's okay! We'll start with a gentle exam and cleaning, then create a treatment plan to restore your oral health. No judgment — just support.

What should I expect at my first visit?

We'll review your health history, take X-rays if needed, perform a thorough exam, and determine the type of cleaning you may need. Together, we'll discuss your goals and create a personalized care plan.

Is dental care safe during pregnancy?

Yes. Preventive care and most dental treatments are safe during pregnancy, though we may postpone certain elective procedures until after delivery.

How can I prevent cavities at home?

Brush twice daily with fluoride toothpaste, floss daily, limit sugary snacks/drinks, and see us regularly for checkups and professional cleanings.
